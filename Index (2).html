Ip Cameras

IP cameras are digital video cameras that transmit data over a network or the internet, offering high-quality video capture, remote access from anywhere, flexible installation options, advanced features like motion detection and night vision, integration with other systems, and scalability. They're widely used for surveillance and monitoring in both residential and commercial settings, providing enhanced security and convenience compared to traditional analog cameras.

Analog Cameras

Analog cameras are traditional video cameras that capture footage using analog signals. They require a direct connection to a recording device or monitor via coaxial cables. These cameras typically offer standard-definition video quality and have limited features compared to digital IP cameras. Analog cameras are often used in basic surveillance setups where simplicity and affordability are prioritized over advanced functionality.
